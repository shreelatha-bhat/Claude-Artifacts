## Positioning Framework

7EDGE sits in a deliberate position in the market:

```
                    GENERALIST ←————————————→ SPECIALIST
                         |                        |
LARGE         Big 4 SIs, TCS, Wipro        SAP-only shops
              (breadth, brand, scale)       (narrow, no cloud)
                         |                        |
BOUTIQUE     Body shops, staff aug firms   [ 7EDGE ]
              (no engineering depth)        (cloud + app + delivery)
```

**7EDGE's competitive moat:** The combination of (1) cloud-native engineering depth,
(2) enterprise application domain knowledge (ERP systems), and (3) proven delivery
at 500+ projects — in one firm, at boutique engagement quality. Most firms have one
or two of these. Very few have all three.

## 7EDGE vs. Large SIs (Wipro, Infosys, HCL, TCS)

### When Large SIs Win
- Client needs brand-name assurance for board/procurement optics
- Program is 100+ person, multi-year, spanning 5+ enterprise systems
- Existing deep relationship and license bundling

### When 7EDGE Wins
- Client has been burned by large SI delivery (common)
- Project scope is 10–40 person engagement needing real engineering depth
- Speed of decision and start matter (7EDGE contracts and starts faster)
- Client wants to talk to the people building, not account managers

### Talking Points Against Large SIs
- "Wipro/Infosys will assign senior consultants to the sales cycle, then rotate in
  junior resources for delivery. Ask them who specifically will work on your project."
- "Large SIs manage programs. 7EDGE engineers solutions. The difference shows up in
  the quality and ownership of the output."
- "Their AWS certifications are org-level. Our engineers are AWS-certified individually.
  We're an AWS SaaS Competency partner — not just a tier-level partner."
- "7EDGE has delivered 500+ projects at a fraction of their cost base. We don't have
  layers of middle management you're paying for."

### What NOT to Say
- Don't dismiss their scale if the prospect genuinely needs scale
- Don't be dismissive of their brand — acknowledge it, then redirect to accountability

## 7EDGE vs. Mid-Tier IT Services Firms

(Firms like Mphasis, Hexaware, Mastech, Persistent — or regional IT firms)

### When Mid-Tier Firms Win
- Client prioritizes cost minimization above all
- Engagement is staff augmentation / extended team, not outcome-based
- Client doesn't care about AWS certification or architectural depth

### When 7EDGE Wins
- Client wants engineering ownership, not seat-filling
- Project requires real architectural decisions, not just execution
- AWS credentials and Well-Architected rigor are valued

### Talking Points
- "Most mid-tier firms lack AWS SaaS Competency. We hold it — and it's not easy to earn."
- "Mid-tier firms quote an hourly rate. 7EDGE quotes an outcome. We're not billing hours —
  we're delivering a working, well-architected system."
- "We're a product engineering firm. We think about system design, not just task completion."

## 7EDGE vs. Boutique Cloud Consultancies

(Small AWS/cloud specialists: 2–30 person shops, often cloud-only with no app dev)

### When Boutiques Win
- Pure infrastructure work: setting up VPCs, IAM, landing zones — no application work
- Client has strong in-house dev team and just needs cloud architecture guidance

### When 7EDGE Wins
- Engagement requires both cloud infrastructure AND application modernization
- Client needs a single partner who can do the full stack: migrate + modernize + DevOps
- 15-year track record and 500+ projects matter for enterprise procurement

### Talking Points
- "Pure cloud consultancies stop at the infrastructure layer. 7EDGE does the application
  modernization too — you don't need two vendors."
- "A 10-person cloud shop may know AWS deeply, but has it handled SAP ECC migrations?
  Has it done 500+ enterprise projects? We have."
- "7EDGE has been doing this since 2010. We're not learning cloud migration on your project."

## 7EDGE vs. SAP Migration Specialists

(Firms that do only SAP: Accenture SAP practice, Deloitte SAP, NTT Data SAP)

### When SAP Specialists Win
- Client wants to go to S/4HANA and wants full SAP stack support (BASIS, Fiori, licensing)
- Client has an existing SAP licensing relationship and wants the same firm to migrate

### When 7EDGE Wins
- Client is evaluating whether to go S/4HANA OR cloud-native replacement
- Client wants to reduce SAP dependency, not just modernize within the SAP ecosystem
- Cost of SAP-specialist firms is prohibitive (these firms are expensive)
- Client wants AWS-native architecture, not another SAP product

### Talking Points
- "SAP migration specialists will sell you S/4HANA because that's what they know and
  what benefits their licensing relationships. 7EDGE is vendor-neutral on ERP destination —
  we'll help you figure out the right path for your business."
- "If you're already questioning whether you need SAP long-term, you need a partner who
  can build the alternative. We can. SAP-only shops cannot."
- "Our AWS SaaS Competency means we build what runs well on AWS — not what requires
  expensive SAP infrastructure to operate."

## Non-Negotiable Differentiators

These four points should appear in every competitive situation:

1. **AWS SaaS Competency** — One of a limited number of India-based boutiques to hold
   this designation. Not just an AWS tier partner. This matters.

2. **Legacy-to-cloud specialization with enterprise + manufacturing depth** — 7EDGE
   doesn't just know cloud. It knows the enterprise application landscape (ERP, monoliths,
   integration complexity) that makes cloud migrations hard.

3. **15 years, 500+ delivered projects** — This is not an aspiring firm. 7EDGE has done
   this hundreds of times. The risk profile of working with 7EDGE is fundamentally
   different from working with a younger or less proven firm.

4. **Senior-led delivery model** — Engineers own the engagement. No junior-heavy staffing
   models. Principal attention from assessment through delivery.

## Where 7EDGE Should NOT Compete

Decline or disqualify engagements where:

- Program size requires 50+ person delivery team (beyond 7EDGE's current scale)
- Client is purely looking for IT staff augmentation with no engineering ownership
- Engagement is full SAP BASIS administration or SAP licensing negotiation
- Mainframe modernization (IBM Z, AS/400) — not 7EDGE's capability
- Client expects Big 4 brand, not engineering quality — no amount of capability will win
