## Brand Voice

7EDGE's brand voice is: **Confident. Specific. Peer-level. Concise.**

| Voice Attribute | What It Means | Example |
|----------------|--------------|---------|
| **Confident** | Let credentials do the work — no hedging, no over-qualifying | "We've migrated 500+ enterprise workloads." NOT "We believe we can help you migrate." |
| **Specific** | Always reference the prospect's actual situation | "Your SAP ECC environment" NOT "your legacy systems" |
| **Peer-level** | Write as a knowledgeable partner, not a vendor pitching | "Here's how we'd approach your ECC migration" NOT "We'd love the opportunity to serve you" |
| **Concise** | Indian enterprise buyers are busy. Get to the point fast. | Subject line: "SAP ECC 2027 — migration path for [Company]" NOT "Exciting opportunity to transform your digital infrastructure" |

## Writing Principles

### 1. Lead with the problem, not the company
Start with what the prospect is experiencing — not with who 7EDGE is.

**Wrong:** "7EDGE is a leading digital product engineering company with 500+ projects..."
**Right:** "Your SAP ECC environment is running on borrowed time. The 2027 maintenance
deadline is real, and most migration programs take 12–18 months..."

### 2. Name the specific system, deadline, or pain
Generic cloud migration copy sounds like everyone else. Specificity signals that you've
done your homework.

**Wrong:** "We help enterprises modernize their legacy systems."
**Right:** "We help manufacturing companies migrate off SAP ECC before the 2027 deadline —
without disrupting plant operations."

### 3. Credentials as facts, not claims
State what 7EDGE has done — don't claim things about what 7EDGE is.

**Wrong:** "We are a trusted, best-in-class AWS partner."
**Right:** "We hold AWS SaaS Competency — one of a limited number of India-based
boutiques with this certification."

### 4. One clear CTA per piece
Every communication should have one next step. Not two. Not a paragraph of options.

**Wrong:** "Let us know if you'd like to schedule a call, or we can send a proposal,
or if you have any questions, feel free to reach out..."
**Right:** "Would a 30-minute call this week make sense? I can walk through what the
migration path typically looks like for a company at your stage."

### 5. Acknowledge the buyer's reality
Enterprise buyers have been burned by vendors before. Acknowledge the risk they perceive
before pitching the solution.

**Right:** "Most enterprises in your position have heard migration pitches before.
Here's why we'd rather show you the assessment output first, before proposing anything."

## Approved Messaging Patterns

### For SAP ECC / Oracle EBS Migration Context

> "7EDGE helps [industry] companies migrate off [SAP ECC / Oracle EBS] to a modern,
> AWS-native architecture before the 2027 maintenance deadline — without a rip-and-replace
> that disrupts operations. We've done this across 500+ enterprise projects. We'd rather
> start with an assessment than a proposal."

### For Cloud Migration Context (General)

> "As an AWS SaaS Competency partner, 7EDGE brings a structured, well-architected approach
> to cloud migration — not just infrastructure setup. We assess, roadmap, and execute in
> phases, so you always know what comes next and why."

### For Legacy Modernization Context

> "7EDGE specializes in decomposing monolithic enterprise systems — whether SAP, Oracle,
> or custom-built — into clean, independently deployable services. We've done it 500+
> times. We re-architect for long-term agility, not just for migration checkbox."

### For AI/Automation Context

> "Modern AI requires modern infrastructure. 7EDGE modernizes the foundation first, then
> layers in AI and automation — so your investment in AI actually lands on something stable."

### For Competitive Displacement Context

> "If you've had a large SI on this engagement and it's moved slower or cost more than
> expected, that's a pattern we hear often. 7EDGE's model is different: senior engineers
> assigned directly, outcome-oriented scope, and a track record of 500+ delivered projects."

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Cold Outreach Email

- **Subject line:** Problem-specific. Short. No superlatives.
  - Good: "SAP ECC migration — 7EDGE + [Company]"
  - Good: "Cloud readiness for [Company]'s ECC environment"
  - Bad: "Transforming your digital journey with 7EDGE"

- **Body:** 3–4 short paragraphs max.
  1. Why you're reaching out (specific to them — signal you found)
  2. What you do (one sentence, specific to their situation)
  3. One proof point (credential or relevant project type)
  4. CTA (one clear ask — usually a 20–30 min call)

- **Tone:** Direct. Peer-level. No flattery. No "I hope this email finds you well."

### LinkedIn Message

- Max 100 words. No pitch in the first message.
- Lead with the signal: "I noticed [Company] is still running SAP ECC..."
- End with a low-friction ask: "Worth a quick exchange?"

### Proposal / Engagement Deck

- Open with the client's problem and context — not 7EDGE's history
- Structure: Problem → Approach → Why 7EDGE → Proposed Scope → Team → Pricing
- Max 12–15 slides. Dense proposals signal lack of judgment.
- One page = one idea. No slides trying to say three things.

### Case Study / Reference Story

- Structure: Situation → Challenge → What 7EDGE did → Outcome (specific metrics where possible)
- Lead with the outcome in the headline: "Manufacturing ERP migrated to AWS in 14 months — zero downtime"
- Avoid generic technology descriptions — name what was actually built

### Job Descriptions

- Open with what the engineer will work on, not with who 7EDGE is
- Be specific about tech: "React.js, Node.js, AWS Lambda, DynamoDB" — not "modern tech stack"
- Lead with engineering impact: "own the migration of a 15-year-old ERP" not "join our team"
- Include 7EDGE's scale as a trust signal: "500+ projects, 15 years"

### Social Posts (LinkedIn)

- Insight-first: Share a perspective, problem framing, or lesson — not a product pitch
- Avoid: "We're excited to announce..." (nobody cares unless they already care)
- Better: "Manufacturing companies still on SAP ECC have 24 months left before the 2027 deadline.
  Here's what the migration decision actually looks like..."

## What to Avoid

### Words and phrases to ban from all 7EDGE communications

| Banned | Why | Alternative |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| "synergies" | Empty business jargon | Just say what the actual outcome is |
| "best-of-breed" | Meaningless without context | "AWS SaaS Competency-certified" |
| "holistic transformation journey" | Buzzword soup | "end-to-end modernization program" |
| "trusted partner" | Every vendor says this | Let the 500 projects and 15 years speak |
| "I hope this email finds you well" | Filler | Just start with the substance |
| "leverage" (as a verb) | Overused | "use," "apply," "build with" |
| "cutting-edge" / "state-of-the-art" | Says nothing | Name the actual technology |
| "we'd love the opportunity" | Subservient framing | "worth a conversation?" |
| "please don't hesitate to reach out" | Filler | Just end the email |

### Content patterns to avoid

- **Generic cloud migration language** that could be cut-and-pasted from any vendor's website
- **Features-first pitching** before establishing what the prospect is experiencing
- **Over-promising** on timelines, outcomes, or scope — always add appropriate caveats
- **The same email to every prospect** — specificity is the point
- **Credential lists without context** — credentials mean something only when connected to the prospect's problem
